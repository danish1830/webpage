Master Thesis

"Detection and Explanation of Regime Shifts in the Climatic Controls of Norwegian Glaciers"

Institution: University of Glasgow, School of Geographical and Earth Sciences

Supervisor: Dr. rer. nat. habil. Sebastian G. Mutz

Research Cluster: Climate Dynamics Lab

Aim

  • Detect & explain regime shifts in glacier‚Äìclimate relationships (pre-2000 vs post-2000).
  • Develop an empirical-statistical model framework for glacier mass balance.

Hypothesis

"The turn of the century marks the transition to a new glacier regime, characterized by a change in the climatic controls on annual mass balances.

Methods

Data sources: ERA5 reanalysis (ECMWF), CMIP6 projections, and glacier mass-balance observations (NVE: Norwegian Water Resources and Energy Directorate).

Part A:

  • Exploratory data analysis of glacier mass-balance time series (1950‚Äì2023).
  • Empirical Orthogonal Function (EOF) analysis to identify dominant modes of variability.
  • Detection of regime shifts using correlation testing, moving-window analyses, and comparing pre-2000 & post-2000 periods.

Part B:

  • Predictor selection using Recursive & Tree-based selection and ensemble cross-validation.
  • Empirical-Statistical Downscaling via pyESD framework with 6 regressors (ARD, RidgeCV, RandomForest, Bagging, Stacking) & 3 selectors (Recursive, Tree-Based, Sequential).
  • Model evaluation using cross-validation and performance metrics (e.g., RMSE, R¬≤).

Results & Findings

Part A:

  • Identified a major regime shift around 2000 for most glaciers.
  • Post-2000 shift in regime: from winter (bw) ‚Üí summer (bs) dominated control.
  • Shift from winter precipitation (NAO) ‚Üí temperature-dominant controls on glacier mass balance.
  • Spatial gradient: maritime glaciers (‚Üë precipitation sensitive) & continental glaciers (‚Üë temperature sensitive).

Weaker NAO (‚Üì P(acc)) + higher T(abl) ‚Üí MOST NEGATIVE BALANCE ON RECORD POST-2000!
